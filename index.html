The future of animal testing.

ABOUT

Animal testing has been around historically for centuries. One of the first written animal testing records date back to the 4th and 3rd centuries BCE with Aristotle and Erasistratus. It is thanks to animal testing that we are able to buy common household products and cosmetics without having to worry about the dangers of harming ourselves. However, development in alternative testing have risen since the past decades, striving to one day replace animal testing such as the Draize test. The Draize test is a method of animal testing that measures the severity of chemical damage on the eyes by studying Rabbit's eyes. Application of chemical solutions are applied to the Rabbit's cornea and then observed over a period of time. Several alternative test methods have been created to steer away from using Rabbits. Below is a timeline on the development and history of animal testing.

  • Origin of Animal Testing

    304 - 258 BCE

    First written records of animal testing were in Ancient Greek. Aristotle and Erasistratus were one of the first to perform experiments.

  • FD&C Act

    1938

    Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act passed, requiring safety testing of drugs and cosmetics. This act permitted the use of animals for testing of these drugs.

  • Draize Test

    1944

    FDA toxicologist John Draize developed the Draize eye irritation test as a toxicoogy test for cosmetic compounds.

  • Alternative Testing

    1989 - 2009

    Research into developing alternative methods to replace Draize tests began and in 2009, two methods were accepted by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development. One of them being the isolated chicken eye test.

  • Animal Revolution

    2013 - present

    European Union banned animal testing for cosmetics and also banned the sale of any new cosmetics tested on animals. More and more companies are switching to animal testing free products.

Alternative Methods

Human Hemoglobin Denaturation
Using human hemoglobin to study protein denaturation caused by chemicals is one valid method that could replace the Draize test. Protein denaturation is one of the most important factors that causes corneal opacity. Corneal opacity indicates scarring of the cornea, a direct sign of eye irritation. Different known irritants and non-irritants compounds were applied to the hemoglobin and studied. Hemoglobin denaturation was determined by comparing absorbance of treated and untreated wells by measuring the peak of maximum absorption. Decreased absorption indicated that the compound was an irritant. This method was able to differentiate between irritant and non-irritants and provide enough information about the potnential corneal damage of different compounds. You can read more about human hemoglobin denaturation here.
Reconstituted Human Corneal Epithelium
This 3D epithelial model was cultivated from human corneal cells and could be a promising method for the predication of eye irritation caused by irritatnts. In a study, 65 cosmetic products and 36 chemical solutions were tested using a simplified toxicokinatic approach. This model was able to predict human ocular response and the data collected from this model correlated with the respective scores in historic Draize tests. You can read more about the reconstituted human corneal epithelium model here.
Isolated Chicken Eye Test
The ICE test uses chicken eyes collected from slaughter houses. 44 chemical compounds were used to study the effects of eye irritation on the chicken eyes. Categories were made to organize the data collected; corneal swelling, corneal opacity, fluorescein retantion of damaged corneal epithelium, and an index score which was a combination of the three. categories. The data collected from this study correlated positively with the past Draize test scores. It was concluded that the ICE test can be a prescreen for the Draize test. You can read more about the isolated chicken eye test here.
Short Time Exposure Test
The STE test is a cytotoxicity test using saline or mineral oil as the test solvent. This test uses rabbit corneal cell line cells and uses cell viabiity at a constant concentration as the endpoint for irritation potential. In this study, 51 chemical solutions were tested and data was collected and examined. The STE test was able to predict the eye irritation potential in 5% test concentration.
